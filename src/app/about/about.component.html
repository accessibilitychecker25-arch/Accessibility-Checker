Accessibility Standards

Auburn University believes in education and creating an environment where everyone can succeed. In April of 2024, the U.S. DOJ released a new rule with a new set of digital accessibility standards that have to be followed. Click here to access Auburn's Statement and response to this.

The DOJ is now using the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA as the official standard. The standard includes:

  • Text alternatives for non-text content (e.g., images, charts)
  • Captions and transcripts for audio and video
  • Keyboard accessibility for all interactive elements
  • Clear navigation and readable content

Our Mission

We help Auburn University ensure that digital content is accessible to all users, including those with disabilities. Our accessibility checker identifies and helps resolve accessibility issues in Word documents to ensure compliance with the new DOJ requirements and WCAG 2.1 AA standards.

How It Works

Simply upload your Word document and our advanced analysis engine will scan for accessibility issues, providing detailed reports with actionable recommendations to improve your document's compliance with Auburn University's accessibility standards.
